The cost is already rising. Every year of delay adds $2 million.
The 2025 petition cited $21.2 million. By January 2026 the city's own consultant, Griffin Structures, put the cost at $37.2 million — a $16 million jump before a single shovel entered the ground. Griffin projects construction costs rising 6% per year. The library cannot break ground until a state housing grant is awarded — a grant the developer has never applied for on this site.

Passing Measure C collects your taxes. It does not build a library.
Construction depends on the developer winning a competitive state housing grant for a site it has never applied for. The same developer applied for a nearby parcel and was rejected in the most recent round. If the grant is denied, taxes are collected — and the library isn't built. There is no guarantee, no backup plan, and no refund.
El Cerrito never applied for the grants Bay Point received
The California State Library's Building Forward program has awarded hundreds of millions of dollars to libraries across the state. Bay Point received $9.9 million from this program. El Cerrito never applied. Instead, 100% of the cost of Measure C falls on El Cerrito property owners through a 30-year escalating parcel tax.
"Bay Point is getting the same library — same county, same BART-station model — and its residents pay nothing extra. El Cerrito never applied for those grants. Why are we being asked to pay for everything ourselves?"
El Cerrito deserves a library.
This plan doesn't deliver one.
