## Fire Department Incident Analysis (El Cerrito, Kensington & Richmond, 2024)

This project comprises two reports analyzing fire department incident data for 2024.
The first focuses on El Cerrito and Kensington; the second adds Richmond for a
regional comparison. Together they examine response times, incident volumes, and
mutual aid patterns across the three jurisdictions.

---

## Part 1 — El Cerrito and Kensington (2024)

### Key Findings

**El Cerrito's west-side station has the fastest response times**
The west-side station consistently outperforms other stations in response time.
Kensington shows the slowest times, likely reflecting the challenges of hilly
terrain and narrow streets in that community.

**Response-time differences are statistically significant**
The variation between stations is not random — statistical testing confirms that
geography and station location produce meaningful differences in response
performance.

## Part 2 — El Cerrito/Kensington vs. Richmond (2024)

### Key Findings

**Richmond stations handle significantly higher incident volumes**
Richmond stations average approximately 1,868 incidents per station compared to
roughly 1,290 for El Cerrito — a substantially higher workload per unit.

**El Cerrito provides more mutual aid to Richmond than it receives**
El Cerrito units respond into Richmond far more often than Richmond units respond
into El Cerrito. This imbalance has implications for El Cerrito's resource
capacity and response times for its own residents.

**Kensington remains the slowest; Richmond is faster on average**
The regional comparison confirms Kensington's structural disadvantage in response
time, while Richmond's larger and more distributed station network produces
faster average times.
