The exemptions available to El Cerrito small businesses under Measure C.
Seniors have a (largely inaccessible) exemption. Businesses have nothing. The tax hits every commercial square foot at the same rate as every home — and rises every year for 30+ years.
1
$0.17 per square foot. Every year. Rising.
The tax rate is identical for commercial and residential properties. There is no small business rate, no hardship exemption, and no cap on how high the rate can go over 30 years. The City Council raises the rate annually by resolution — no voter approval required. The table below shows what typical El Cerrito business footprints pay.

2
If you lease your space, you likely pay this anyway.
Most El Cerrito commercial leases are triple-net (NNN) — meaning tenants pay property taxes, insurance, and maintenance on top of base rent. If your landlord's parcel tax bill rises every year for 30 years, that cost flows directly into your occupancy expenses. Whether you own or lease, Measure C adds to your bottom line — without any exemption, any benefit, or any recourse.
No exemption for small businesses — not even a token one
Measure C includes a senior exemption — inaccessible in practice, but at least it exists on paper. There is no equivalent provision for small businesses, sole proprietors, or commercial tenants. El Cerrito has 223 commercial parcels covering nearly two million square feet. Every one of them pays the full rate, with no pathway to relief.
3
You pay starting December 2026. The library opens in 2032 at the earliest.
Whatever foot traffic or community benefit a new library might generate for local businesses — that is six years away at minimum, and only if a competitive state grant is awarded to a developer who has never applied for that grant on this site. The same developer applied for a nearby parcel and was rejected in the most recent round. You pay now. The benefit, if it ever arrives, arrives later.

Measure C is one of several new taxes hitting businesses in 2026.
El Cerrito businesses already face some of the highest combined sales tax rates in California. Measure C adds an annual parcel tax on top of a proposed Contra Costa County healthcare sales tax increase, a regional transit measure, and a community college bond — all landing in the same 12-month period. Each measure looks modest in isolation. The stack does not.
El Cerrito has 223 commercial parcels covering 1.93 million square feet of building area. At the initial maximum rate, commercial properties collectively pay roughly $328,000 per year toward this tax — with no exemption, no sunset on rate increases, and no guarantee the library is ever built.
"I run a small restaurant on San Pablo. My margins are tight. This tax hits my space the same as every house in El Cerrito — with no exemption and no break. I'm being asked to fund a library that won't open for six years, if ever, while I pay every month starting now."
No exemption. No break. No vote.
