## Measure C — El Cerrito Library Project Analysis

Measure C is a proposed 30-year parcel tax on the June 2, 2026 El Cerrito ballot
to fund construction and operation of a new library. This section presents
independent data analysis of the project's costs, financing structure, timeline,
and fiscal risk.

---

### Key Findings

**Construction cost increased 75% before a single brick was laid**
The 2025 citizen petition cited a construction cost of $21.2 million. By January
2026, Griffin Structures' estimate had risen to $37.2 million — a $16 million
increase before design began or any contracts were signed.

**The library depends on a competitive state grant that has never been applied for**
Construction cannot proceed without an Affordable Housing and Sustainable
Communities (AHSC) grant from the state. The proposed site — Parcel C West —
has never submitted an application. The adjacent Parcel C East applied and was
rejected in Round 9 (2024).

**Tax revenue is insufficient to cover both debt service and operating costs**
Independent financial analysis shows the parcel tax revenue cannot support
investment-grade borrowing. The ballot language caps the debt service coverage
ratio at 1.15x, which corresponds to junk bond status.

**The earliest realistic opening date is 2032**
Tax collection begins December 2026. The grant process, design, and construction
timeline push the earliest possible opening to approximately 2032.
