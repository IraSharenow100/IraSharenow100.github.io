## Madison Housing Analysis — Report 2: City and Suburbs (1994–2024)

Report 1 established the long-term price and market trends for the City of Madison.
This report expands the scope to include surrounding suburban communities, allowing
a direct comparison of price levels, home sizes, and appreciation rates between
the city and its suburbs over thirty years.

The combined dataset covers 134,000+ single-family home sales across more than
three decades, with 48 variables per transaction.

---

### Key Findings

**Suburban homes are substantially larger**
Suburban homes average 200–300 square feet more finished space than Madison homes,
reflecting the lower land costs and newer construction stock found outside the city.

**Suburban homes carry a consistent price premium in absolute terms**
When measured by total sale price, suburban homes are generally more expensive than
city homes — driven largely by their larger size.

**Madison commands higher price per square foot**
Despite lower absolute prices, Madison homes sell at a higher price per square foot
than suburban homes. This reflects the premium buyers place on walkability, proximity
to the university, and urban amenities.

**Long-term appreciation is similar across both markets**
Both the city and suburbs show comparable compounded annual appreciation rates over
the full 30-year period, suggesting that location within the metro area matters more
for price level than for long-term growth rate.
