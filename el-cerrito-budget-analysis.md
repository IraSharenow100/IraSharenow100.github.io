## El Cerrito City Budget Analysis

This project analyzes El Cerrito's General Fund over multiple years, examining
revenues, expenditures, reserves, and pension costs. The goal is to assess the
city's long-term fiscal health using publicly available budget documents.

---

### Key Findings

**Structural deficits and overspending**
Expenditures have repeatedly exceeded revenues, producing recurring shortfalls
that are not sustainable over the long term without service cuts or new revenue.

**Sharp increases in fire and administrative costs**
Fire department costs and general administrative expenses have grown
disproportionately relative to other departments, consuming an increasing share
of the General Fund.

**Declining reserves despite strong revenue growth**
Even during periods of healthy sales tax and property tax revenue growth, the
city's reserve balances declined — a sign that spending growth has outpaced
revenue gains.

**Growing CalPERS pension payment burden**
Required pension payments to CalPERS have increased steadily, leaving less
budget capacity for capital investment, maintenance, and services. See also the
[CalPERS Pension Liability Analysis](/calpers-ual-analysis/).

---

### Credit Rating Trend

El Cerrito's credit rating over time reflects these fiscal pressures.
A declining rating signals increased borrowing costs and reduced financial
flexibility for the city.
