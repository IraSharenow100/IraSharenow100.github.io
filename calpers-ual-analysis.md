## CalPERS Pension Liability — El Cerrito

El Cerrito's **CalPERS Unfunded Accrued Liability (UAL)** has reached crisis levels,
crowding out spending on basic city services.

In 2017, Stanford researcher **Joe Nation** warned that rising CalPERS costs would
soon displace essential local services. El Cerrito did not act — and that warning
has become reality.

By 2024, the City Council could not afford:

- A replacement fire engine
- Required fire safety gear
- Full repairs to the city's public swimming pool

In the same period, El Cerrito made its **minimum required UAL payment** to CalPERS
of **$7,763,945**. Because CalPERS relies on optimistic investment return assumptions,
paying only the minimum has allowed the debt to grow substantially over time.
